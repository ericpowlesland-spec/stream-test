Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about working with Nexus Studio.

General
What exactly does Nexus Studio do?

Nexus Studio is a full-service digital studio specialising in web development, UI/UX design, brand identity, and digital strategy. We partner with startups, growing businesses, and enterprise organisations to bring ambitious digital products to life.

From a single landing page to a complex SaaS platform, we handle the full journey — discovery, design, engineering, launch, and ongoing support.

Who are your typical clients?

Our clients range from early-stage startups seeking their first product to established companies looking to modernise legacy systems. We've worked across industries including fintech, health tech, e-commerce, education, media, and enterprise SaaS.

What they share in common: they care deeply about quality, user experience, and long-term outcomes — not just shipping fast.

Where is Nexus Studio based, and do you work remotely?

Our headquarters are in San Francisco, with team members distributed across Europe and Asia. We work with clients worldwide and are fully set up for remote collaboration — async communication, video standups, shared project boards, and documented everything.

If you prefer in-person engagement, we can arrange on-site workshops and strategy sessions for clients in the Bay Area.

What makes Nexus different from a freelancer or a large agency?

Freelancers offer flexibility but limited capacity and breadth. Large agencies bring resources but often slow communication, rotating teams, and padded invoices. Nexus sits in the sweet spot: a dedicated, senior team that's small enough to genuinely care, large enough to tackle complex projects end-to-end.

You always work with the actual people doing the work — no account manager relay race.

Process & Timeline
What does your typical project process look like?

Every project follows our four-phase process:

1. Discovery — We dig into your goals, users, and constraints. This typically takes 1–2 weeks and results in a scoped project brief and technical architecture outline.

2. Design — Wireframes, then high-fidelity UI designs, reviewed and iterated with you until they're right.

3. Build — Engineering sprints with weekly demos so you always know where things stand.

4. Launch & Handoff — QA, deployment, documentation, and a handoff session so your team is fully empowered.

How long does a project typically take?

It depends on scope. A focused marketing site or redesign typically runs 4–8 weeks. A full product build (design system + web app + integrations) is usually 12–24 weeks.

We'll give you a realistic estimate during the discovery phase — one that accounts for review cycles, not just production time. We don't make promises we can't keep.

How involved do I need to be during the project?

We ask for a dedicated point of contact on your side who can review deliverables and make decisions. In practice, this is usually 2–4 hours per week for most clients — primarily attending demos, giving feedback, and approving milestones.

We handle the heavy lifting; we just need you engaged enough so the output actually reflects your vision.

Can you work with our existing in-house team?

Absolutely. We frequently collaborate alongside in-house engineers, designers, and product managers. We're flexible about where we plug in — leading a workstream, augmenting capacity, or acting as a technical advisor and reviewer.

We're respectful of existing team dynamics and do our best to integrate, not disrupt.

Do you offer rush or expedited timelines?

In some cases, yes. Rush work is possible when our schedule allows and the scope is well-defined. Expedited timelines typically carry a surcharge of 20–40% depending on how compressed the timeline is.

Contact us early — the sooner we know about a deadline, the better we can plan for it.

Pricing & Payment
How are projects priced?

We offer two main models:

Fixed-scope: A defined price for a defined deliverable. Best for projects with clear requirements and limited change risk.

Time & materials: A monthly retainer or hourly rate. Best for iterative product work, ongoing development, or exploratory projects where scope evolves.

We'll recommend the right model for your situation during the scoping call.

What's your typical project budget range?

Our projects typically start at $15,000 for focused engagements (a landing page, a brand refresh, a single feature). Full product builds usually range from $50,000 to $250,000+ depending on complexity.

We're transparent about this upfront because we'd rather have an honest conversation early than waste your time.

What are your payment terms?

For fixed-scope projects: 40% upfront, 30% at the midpoint milestone, and 30% on delivery.

For retainers: invoiced monthly in advance, net 14 days.

We accept wire transfer, ACH, and major credit cards. Larger contracts can be structured with custom payment schedules.

Do you offer discounts for startups or non-profits?

We have a small number of reduced-rate spots each year for early-stage startups and non-profit organisations whose missions we believe in. These are competitive — reach out through our contact page and tell us about your work.

We also offer equity arrangements on a case-by-case basis for pre-revenue startups.

What happens if the scope changes mid-project?

Scope changes happen — we expect them. When they do, we raise a change order that outlines the additional work, cost, and any timeline impact. You decide whether to proceed.

We track scope carefully and flag drift early, so there are never surprises on the final invoice.

Technical
What technologies do you work with?

Our frontend stack is primarily React (with Next.js for server-rendered or static sites) and TypeScript. For design systems we use Figma. On the backend, we're comfortable with Node.js, Python, Go, and PostgreSQL/MongoDB depending on the use case.

We also have deep experience with headless CMS platforms (Contentful, Sanity, Strapi), cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Vercel), and third-party integrations (Stripe, Twilio, HubSpot, and many more).

Do you build mobile apps?

We build cross-platform mobile apps using React Native, which allows us to ship on iOS and Android from a shared codebase. For projects that require native-only performance or platform-specific APIs, we partner with specialist native developers.

We'll always be upfront about which approach is right for your product.

Will my site be accessible and SEO-friendly?

Yes, both are non-negotiable for us. We build to WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards as a baseline and conduct accessibility audits before launch. Our sites are semantic HTML-first, which is also the foundation of good SEO.

For clients on growth-stage products, we offer dedicated SEO audits and technical SEO implementation as an add-on service.

Who owns the code and designs at the end of a project?

You do. Upon final payment, full ownership of all source code, design files, and assets transfers to you with no strings attached. We retain the right to reference the work in our portfolio unless you request otherwise under an NDA.

Can you help us migrate from our current platform?

Yes. Migrations are a significant part of our work — from legacy WordPress sites to custom CMSes, from monolith backends to microservices, from one cloud provider to another. We always start with a thorough audit and migration plan before touching production systems.

Support & Ongoing Work
Do you offer ongoing maintenance after launch?

Yes. We offer monthly retainer packages for ongoing maintenance, monitoring, and iterative improvements. Plans start at a few hours per month for simple sites and scale up to dedicated development capacity for active products.

See our Pricing page for details on our support tiers.

What's your response time for urgent issues?

For clients on a support retainer, we guarantee a 4-hour response for critical issues during business hours (9am–6pm Pacific, Mon–Fri). Our Priority support tier includes 24/7 incident response with a 1-hour SLA for P0 outages.

For clients without a retainer, we do our best to respond quickly but cannot guarantee specific SLAs.

Will you hand off the project so our team can manage it?

Absolutely — a complete handoff is standard in every engagement. This includes a documented codebase, a recorded walkthrough, runbooks for common operations, and a live Q&A session with your team.

We want you to feel confident owning and evolving the product independently, even if you choose to continue working with us too.

How do I get started?

The easiest way is to fill out our contact form with a brief description of your project. We'll review it within one business day and schedule a free 30-minute discovery call to learn more and see if we're a good fit.

No pitch decks, no pressure — just a straightforward conversation.
